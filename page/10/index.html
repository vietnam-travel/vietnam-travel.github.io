The time I went the sky couldn’t possibly get more foggy!
This was the site I was aiming for when I set off on my bike yesterday (Day 18 – 6/04/19) I originally thought you could ride to the top by small windy roads but that’s not the case. It is possible but they don’t allow it (Correct me I’m wrong)
I’m actually glad I couldn’t ride up there as I ended up experiencing the highlight of my trip! The Ba Na Hills Cable Car *** Longest in the world + top 10 cable car routes on earth, as featured by CNN ***
👉 https://www.facebook.com/154943102119258/posts/163611951252373?sfns=xmwa
It was busy 12:30pm, I didn’t time my visit there properly if I was to avoid the mass crowds and on reflection….yeah I would have got the shots with hardly anyone on the bridge, but you can’t tell with the weather. That would also mean being ready to cable car it at 7am! I’m not too fussed it’s all good. The cable car journey was epic and I’ll remember that for ever
You see all those wowing photos of the bridge on the net, but rarely do you see the ones where’s it busy and very cloudy/foggy/misty…..call it what you like
I feel that I still took some goodens and it gives you all a teaser especially for anyone planning to go also
My bike ride yesterday to Hoi An and the Cable Car 👉 https://www.facebook.com/154943102119258/posts/163543344592567?sfns=xmwa

Day 22 – 11/04/19 🏍 The photos you’ve all been waiting for!
A selection on my best shots from 5 days back
I’d been looking forward to this since I arrived in Vietnam and had made a mental note ever since
I watched the Top Gear Vietnam Special (2008), in which the presenters waxed lyrical about the pass, inspiring a generation of travellers to take to the road on two wheels and hit the Hai Van Pass
From Da Nang to Hue which was where I’d be stopping for a few days (See maps pic 23 & 24) was 105km and the Pass 21km. The whole trip to Hue took me 5+ hours as I stopped many times for photos, taking in some fresh air and also a beach stop off the beaten track
The Pass took me 1hr 20mins and on the way back I ripped through it in only 26minutes. Total time Hue to Da Nang coming back was 2hrs 20 minutes
A bit about the pass; It’s a geographic and political boundary between ancient kingdoms, as a climatic divide between the tropical south and the subtropical north. It was also a strategic military post during times of war, both ancient and modern
The most famous road in Vietnam, the Hai Van Pass, a scenic route winding around a mountainside above the East Sea on the central coast

Day 16 – 05/04/19 🏯 Had a lazy day and paid merit #travelwithtrev
Didn’t do much today folks but I did ride up to Chua Linh Temple 15 minutes (12km) from Da Nang Central
I rode past here yesterday but my time was short to Temple it + ride to the end of the peninsular
Stunning views over the bay with Da Nangs high rises in the distance. Beautiful temple grounds as you can see….. and going around 4:30pm means you can see the area with blue skies and by 5:45pm the sun is starting to set with the sky alight!
I’m off to pick up a motorcross bike this evening and tomorrow I ride to Hue, which was once Vietnam’s capital city. The journey will take me on the Hai Van Pass, made famous and featured on Top Gear back in 2008. Really looking forward to this and I highly doubt it will be a disappointment
